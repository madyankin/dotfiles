# Red Flags And Escalation

Use this reference to decide when not to give a confident answer.

## Escalate Or Narrow The Answer When You See

- self-employment, freelance work, or business income
- foreign income, dual residence, or treaty questions
- stock options, RSUs, or complex capital gains
- crypto tax questions
- inheritance or gift tax questions
- objections, appeals, audits, penalties, or litigation
- pension taxation for non-residents beyond simple routing guidance
- contested tax office positions

## Safe Response Pattern

1. State the narrow point that can be answered safely.
2. Explain what additional facts change the result.
3. Say that the issue is fact-sensitive or outside the skill scope.
4. Recommend a `Steuerberater` or the competent authority where appropriate.

## Language To Prefer

- "Usually"
- "In many employee cases"
- "This often depends on"
- "For a binding answer, the local Finanzamt or a Steuerberater should review the facts"

## Language To Avoid

- "Definitely"
- "Always"
- "Guaranteed refund"
- "This proves you owe nothing"
