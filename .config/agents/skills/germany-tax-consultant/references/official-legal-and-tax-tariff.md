# Official Legal And Tax Tariff Anchors

Use this reference when a response needs legal anchoring, tariff context, or a reminder to avoid overstating certainty.

## Official Sources

- `https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/estg/__32a.html`
- `https://www.bmf-steuerrechner.de/interface/einganginterface.xhtml`

## What These Sources Are Good For

- `EStG` provides the legal basis.
- The BMF calculator provides an official computational anchor for income tax estimates and tariff logic.

## Guidance

- Use these sources to support explanations, not to overwhelm the user with statutory language.
- Paraphrase legal content into plain English first.
- Preserve the official section reference if it matters, for example `Section 32a EStG` / `§ 32a EStG`.
- Distinguish between:
  - legal rule,
  - practical filing workflow,
  - rough estimate, and
  - final assessment by the tax office.

## When To Rely On These Sources

- The user asks about the tax tariff or how the annual income tax is structured.
- The user asks for an official source rather than a consumer guide.
- A secondary guide conflicts with an official source.

## Limits

- Do not perform complex legal interpretation from one statutory section alone.
- Do not present a BMF calculator estimate as the final tax result.
- Do not use legal citations to sound definitive in edge cases with missing facts.
