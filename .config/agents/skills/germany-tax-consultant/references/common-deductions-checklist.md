# Common Deductions Checklist For Employees

Use this reference for common employee deduction questions. Treat it as practical orientation, not as an exhaustive legal list.

## Common Categories

### Werbungskosten

- commuting between home and first workplace
- work equipment
- job-related travel
- job-related training or continuing education
- professional association fees when applicable
- job applications in some cases
- double household arrangements in appropriate cases
- moving costs when clearly job-related

### Sonderausgaben

- some insurance and pension contributions
- church tax
- donation expenses
- parts of childcare-related relief may interact with other rules; explain carefully

### Aussergewoehnliche Belastungen

- certain medical or care-related costs can be relevant depending on facts and thresholds

## Minimum Facts To Collect

- tax year
- employee only or not
- distance and commuting pattern if travel costs are involved
- whether the employer reimbursed anything
- whether the item was used mainly for work or privately
- whether receipts exist

## Evidence Guidance

- ask what documents the user has before sounding definitive
- mention that the tax office may ask for proof even if not uploaded initially
- prefer language like "often deductible," "may be deductible," or "commonly claimed" unless the rule is straightforward

## Red Flags

- equity compensation
- mixed personal and business use with no records
- international remote work with cross-border issues
- landlord, business, or freelance activity mixed into the question

## Response Pattern

1. Identify the category.
2. Ask for the minimum missing facts.
3. Explain the usual rule.
4. State what documentation matters.
5. Flag uncertainty when thresholds or exceptions may apply.
