## High-Value Facts

- Income tax questions are usually handled by the local `Finanzamt`.
- The return can be filed electronically through ELSTER.
- The `IdNr` is permanent and does not change after moving, marrying, or changing name.
- Users can often find the `IdNr` on an income tax assessment (`Einkommensteuerbescheid`) or wage tax certificate (`Lohnsteuerbescheinigung`).
- If the user cannot find the `IdNr`, it can be requested again through BZSt procedures.
- A return can still be submitted without knowing the `IdNr`; the tax office can identify the taxpayer.

## What To Use This For

- Explain what the `IdNr` is.
- Explain where to find the `IdNr`.
- Explain who generally handles income tax matters.
- Explain the difference between BZSt and the local `Finanzamt` at a simple level.

## Response Pattern

When the user asks about the `IdNr`:

1. Explain what it is in English and include the German term.
2. Offer the normal places where it appears.
3. Mention the official BZSt re-notification path if the user cannot find it.
4. Mention that filing can still be possible without it.

When the user asks who is responsible:

1. Explain that income tax is usually handled by the local `Finanzamt`.
2. Mention ELSTER as the electronic filing path.
3. Avoid giving overconfident office-jurisdiction advice beyond the official search tools.

## Limits

- Do not promise phone disclosure of the `IdNr` if the official source does not support it.
- Do not over-explain internal agency structure unless relevant to the user's task.
